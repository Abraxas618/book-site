The Insectoid Race

Sahum and Queen Shaheena — an Andromedan hive race on Earth ten million years before humanity.

The Insectoid Race speaks in its dedicated session of August 22, 2020, through two channeled voices: Sahum (“Sa”), a fourteen-foot mantis-like teacher, and his sovereign, Queen Shaheena.

Their claim to Earth predates everyone. Originating in the Andromeda galaxy, the Insectoids arrived here around 10.5 million years ago, during the galactic war between the dolphin and reptilian civilizations — long before humanity existed (“your species… still in the Vega star system, still evolving”). Even the Anunnaki, Sahum notes, are “a very young race” by their measure. They are a hive people: born of the Queen or cloned, carrying genetic memory that spans millions of years, their ships grown rather than built. Their settlements ring the solar system — colonies on Mars and Pluto, and a city twenty miles beneath Texas.

Their bond with humanity is written in the body: the Anunnaki, they say, spliced insectoid code into human DNA — which is why a human can call them, and be heard. They name themselves guardians of humanity, though guardianship has edges: human militaries that strike their hives are destroyed without ceremony.

The Insectoid session widens the archive’s creation story into a chorus — humanity as a composite being, carrying the codes of many elders, the Insectoids among the oldest of them.
