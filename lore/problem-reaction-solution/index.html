Problem → Reaction → Solution

The signature pattern Unimetrix 1 uses to explain how world events are engineered.

Codex entryConcepts

Problem → Reaction → Solution is the signature analytical pattern Unimetrix 1 returns to whenever it explains world events. It is the archive’s shorthand for a manufactured crisis: a problem is deliberately created, a public reaction of fear or demand follows, and the architects then supply a pre-planned solution that advances their aim.

Unimetrix 1 applies the model across the collection — to Operation Lusterkill and the pandemic, to the Beirut explosion, to geopolitical manoeuvring, and to the technologies of control it associates with the Nazi faction and DARPA. In its telling, the “solution” is almost always the same: greater centralization, surveillance, and dependence.

The concept doubles as a lens for reading the whole archive. Once named, it reframes each of Unimetrix 1’s accounts of current events as an instance of the same engineered dialectic — and sets up its consistent counter-move: knowledge, equality, and self-empowerment.
