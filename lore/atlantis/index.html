Atlantis

The war that ended the golden age — Atlantis versus Agartha's Kingdom of Rama, and the Fall that shrank humanity.

Codex entryPlaces & Phenomena

Atlantis, in the archive’s telling, is not merely a lost city — it is one side of the war that ended Earth’s golden age. The September 4, 2020 session, delivered by a guardian being called Asha of the “Asha Dimension Heavenly Lineage,” gives the fullest account.

Before the Fall — the session places it around 15,300 BC — Earth was engineered paradise: a controlled tropical world, 70–80°F everywhere, without storms, sheltered under a two-layer ice shield and threaded with the pyramid and ley-line grids built by the ancient lineages (the archive credits the Avian race among the builders). Its people were giant light-beings — the tall generations of the Anunnaki inheritance.

Then the Atlantis–Egyptian empire went to war with the Agartha Kingdom of Rama, fighting with space fleets. The grid network shattered; the ice shield collapsed; cosmic radiation poured in — and it mutated human DNA, severed the light body, and shrank humanity to a tenth of its size, cutting lifespan and mind with it. This is the Fall the archive returns to everywhere: the reason abilities are dormant, the reason the Rainbow Light Body must be restored rather than invented.

The interview sessions preserve a second account of the end: one of Earth’s two moons falling onto the continent — after which “90% of the population exoded to the star systems Zeta 1 and Zeta 2,” the military remnant withdrawing to North America and Asia. They add texture to the empire itself: Atlantean ships of living granite, flown by pilots who merged their molecular bodies with the stone. Two tellings — war or moonfall — one Fall; the archive keeps both as spoken.

Asha’s lineage, the session says, is decreed to guide that restoration — humanity back to giant light-body form, the world back to garden. The April 2020 material adds a coda: old Atlantis fell in northeast Africa, and the United States, in the sessions’ phrase, is the “New Atlantis” — the empire’s echo, replaying its choices. Alongside Lemuria and Agartha, Atlantis anchors the third epoch of the chronicle: the golden age, and how it was lost.
