The Sega (Kosol's Dream Teachers)

The conical-helmeted beings Kosol Ouch says trained him across a 32-year dream at Angkor Wat — the origin of his mission and his devices.

Codex entryBeings & Races

The Sega — also called the Esiad — are, in Kosol Ouch’s own account, the beings who set his whole path in motion. He describes them in the August 10, 2020 session: at Angkor Wat in the year 2000, on his honeymoon, he fell asleep in the temple’s heat and entered what he calls a “daydream” that lasted thirty-two years while only twenty minutes passed in this world.

In that dreamworld, he says, “I received the training & knowledge from the people… I call them the Sega (or Esiad.) And they were black, but they dressed in spacesuit-types with conical helmets, like the people from the 3D Hieroglyphics… in the Khmer tradition and Angkor Wat Temple.” They trained him, by his telling, “in all kinds of technologies: in Spiritual technologies, and Quantum technology, and Consciousness training” — before placing him in “a Crystal Coffin, like a tomb,” where a computer voice announced he was being sent back. He adds one disarming detail: among them he “saw Captain Picard,” and “Patrick was the leader of that particular world.”

Whatever one makes of it, this is the founding story the archive rests on: Kosol says he woke with “an injunction to build this stuff” he had seen — the seashell-and-metal Source-Coil Device that channels the sessions, the consciousness-run craft, the books, and the practice he would name Baramay. The Sega are the archive’s first cause in its own words — the teachers behind the teacher, recorded as he tells it.
