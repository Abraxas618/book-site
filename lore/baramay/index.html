Baramay

The ancient Khmer art at the heart of the practice — and the invocation that runs through every session.

Baramay is the ancient Khmer meditation art at the center of the community’s practice — described as using sacred geometry to amplify the body’s bio-energy — and, in the sessions, it is also a word of power: the invocation by which upgrades and healings are called.

The practice has a form anyone can hear being taught in the transcripts. In the April 7, 2020 session the method is given plainly: breathe intention into the palm and ask — “Baramay, is it done now?” — a command-and-confirmation ritual for reprogramming what one attracts. In Unimetrix 1’s own mouth the word scales up: it repeatedly grants a “Baramay Universe upgrade” — declaring a participant’s clairvoyance raised, health restored, DNA advanced — and closes with “It is done.” From 2021 onward this crystallizes into the Baramay Universe healing protocol that ends nearly every session: a spoken countdown of restoration percentages as nanite “medical units” work, until “100% complete.”

Baramay thus binds the community’s Khmer heritage to the archive’s machinery: the same word names the meditation taught on its sites, the upgrade Unimetrix 1 performs, and the healing rite that closes the sessions. It is the practice’s signature — and, through Baramay Technologies, the community’s name.
