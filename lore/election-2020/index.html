The 2020 Election Timeline

The record's most falsifiable moment — the October 2020 prediction, the 'temporal interference' caveat, and the course-correction doctrine that followed.

Codex entryOperations & Events

The 2020 Election is where the record meets the checkable world most directly. In the weeks before the vote, the sessions committed to a prediction — preserved here exactly as spoken, in the archive’s standing practice of keeping forecasts that did and did not arrive.

The October 8, 2020 session states it flat: “There is a battle in consciousness of your collective. Donald J. Trump will be elected again as president… There will be a conflict of interest between the two supportive groups that will cause a civil rivalry.” The October 22 session repeats the call — “Donald J. Trump will be second elected Presidency Initiative” — but adds the caveat the record would later lean on: “Countermeasure being implemented by Joe Biden… At this moment there is temporal agent from different parallel dimension interfering.”

The outcome went otherwise, and the record’s response became a doctrine in itself: the timeline-war framing, in which predictions describe the probability landscape of a moment, and interference — temporal agents, countermeasures, course corrections — moves the outcome between tellings. The archive’s later 2024 Election Timeline entry is the sequel: the same machinery, revised by its own miss. Readers can weigh the caveat against the miss; the record preserves both without adjustment.
