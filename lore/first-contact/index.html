First Contact

The open meeting of humanity and the galactic community — a date the sessions set, defer, and reset.

Codex entryOperations & Events

First Contact — open, acknowledged meeting between humanity and the galactic community — is the event toward which the whole archive points, and its schedule can be traced across the sessions like a moving star.

The date is first fixed in mid-2023: April 16, 2024 — repeated through the NESARA/GESARA session and into the April 2024 material, where it grows vivid: contact at 5 AM Texas time, with “144 million atmospheric motherships” — craft the size of cities and moons — announced through every religious network at once, ETs living among humans within months. When April 2024 passes without landings, the sessions restage the plan: visible contact through 2025, more in 2026, colonization in 2027 — and the final session pins it precisely: March 25, 2027. The strategy softens with the date: from mass arrival to a quiet seeding of artifacts, like the Buga Sphere, left for humanity to reverse-engineer.

In the sessions’ own logic, the moving date is not failure but course correction — the timeline being continuously edited by the Interstellar Alliance so that contact arrives on a prepared world rather than an unready one. The April 2024 eclipse, digital currency, disclosure, and the group’s own upgrades are all framed as preparation.

First Contact is the destination the archive never stops describing: the moment its two worlds — the one in the transcripts and the one outside them — are promised to meet.
