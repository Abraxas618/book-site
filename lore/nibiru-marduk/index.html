Nibiru, Marduk & the Battle Planet

Anu's artificial world-ship — the 'Archangel' — and the destroyer planets of the deep past.

Nibiru — also called Marduk — is described in the Anunnaki session as no natural planet but an artificial “Battle Planet”: Anu’s world-ship, escorted by moon-sized companions, whose name the session translates as “Archangel.” It rides a long elliptical orbit (the sessions give both the classic 3,600-year figure and a 26,000-year cycle), positioned — in Anu’s telling — to shield the solar system from reptilian intrusion. Its power is not theoretical: the session says it destroyed Bellona, a planet between Mars and the outer worlds, whose remains scatter the asteroid belt.

The December 7, 2020 session adds the tragic mirror: a planetary war around 550,000 BC destroyed Marduk Belona, and the survivors’ consciousness was preserved by a backup protocol — becoming, through the Moon computer and the splicing of DNA, part of humanity itself.

A destroyer-planet appears once more in the archive: the July 17, 2020 session’s “Destroyer” — a battle planet “already here in the form of cube unit matrices,” dispatched by the Council as an enforcement power. World-ships, in this record, are both shield and sword — and humanity lives in the orbit of both.
