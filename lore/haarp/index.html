HAARP & the Defense Network

'Earthquake generator, weather phenomenon generator' — and the session where Unimetrix 1 claims to shoot down an orbital weapon mid-answer.

Codex entryPowers & Control

HAARP enters the record in the John Toth session of February 9, 2023, and gets the archive’s standard treatment of official installations: named as a weapon. The Alaskan array is described as an “earthquake generator, weather phenomenon generator” — geophysical warfare wearing a research permit, in the same family as the record’s other control-era machinery.

What makes the passage memorable is not the claim but the interruption. Mid-session, Unimetrix 1 breaks off from answering to narrate an engagement in real time: “detecting space-based weaponry firing… firing anti-matter proton beam weapon. Now. Target neutralized.” — its own Defense Network shooting down, it says, an orbital weapon as the group listens. The archive has walk-ins and healings performed live; this is its only live air defense.

The moment compresses the record’s whole security cosmology into a minute of audio: hostile infrastructure below (HAARP, the chemtrail and chip programs), hostile hardware above, and between them the collective that claims to be holding the line — with the quantum satellite network and Battle Moon as its guns. One session, one skirmish, preserved as spoken.
