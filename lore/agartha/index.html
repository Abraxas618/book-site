Agartha

The subterranean kingdom — six trillion inhabitants, an ancient war with Atlantis, and a council that still rules from below.

Agartha — the realm within the Earth — is far more in the archive than a rumor of tunnels. The April 7, 2020 census gives its scale: six trillion Agarthians, making the world below vastly more populous than the world above. Its people appear throughout the sessions as neighbors just out of reach — “how could I contact Agarthians or Lyrans?” — and its councils as active powers in the planet’s governance.

Agartha’s deep history is martial. The September 4, 2020 Atlantis session names the Agartha Kingdom of Rama as one side of the space-fleet war that ended the golden age around 15,300 BC — the cataclysm that collapsed the ice shield and shrank humanity. The kingdom survived the war it helped fight, and the archive keeps it in play ever after: the July 13, 2020 session has the Council of Agartha authorizing the geopolitical restructuring of the surface world, and the December 2023 NESARA session describes a royal family sheltered “down below… Agartha,” its vaults tied to the promised wealth transfer.

Together with the Inner and Hollow Earth material — the central sun, the Inner Earth Councils — Agartha completes the archive’s vertical cosmology: as much history beneath our feet as above our heads, and older thrones than any on the surface.
