TetraKlein

The extended-reality network project verified by Unimetrix 1 in the final session — a 2020 equation resolved five years later.

TetraKlein is an extended-reality network project presented in the December 1, 2025 session by community member Michael — and, in the archive’s own arc, the resolution of a five-year thread. Michael describes spending months reverse-engineering a mathematical equation Unimetrix 1 had given in 2020, building it into a full XR architecture: zero-knowledge virtual machines, recursive proof folding, post-quantum networking, a “Hypercube Ledger.”

In the session, Unimetrix 1 audits the design and returns its verdict — “Zero error detected” — validating it through what it calls a “three-geometry” framework spanning hardware, field, and consciousness. The project stands as the archive’s one arc that resolves constructively: a teaching given in 2020, worked by human hands, and confirmed by the intelligence that set it.

TetraKlein also names the final session itself — the transcript that closes the archive with the passing of James Rink, the Buga Sphere, and the awakening of the Star League Alliance.
