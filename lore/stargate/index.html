Stargates & Stargate Travel

Portals in temples and minds — Angkor Wat's quantum network, and the practice of remote stargate travel.

Stargates appear in the archive as technology, as geography, and as practice — and their great terrestrial anchor is Angkor Wat. The March 2020 sessions describe the Khmer temple complex as “a quantum computer chip network for planetary communication acceleration” — built on the ley-line grid with “pyramidal mathematic geometry” to open “quantum neurolink” portals. From it, the sessions say, one can reach the Draconian systems, the Zeta Reticuli systems, and Sirius — and it was through Angkor Wat’s central tower that Kosol Ouch himself, by his account, was taken into the far future.

As practice, the archive teaches “remote stargate travel” — consciousness-side transit that Unimetrix 1 offers to enhance (“we will upgrade you… to level A clairvoyant ability”). As infrastructure, gates thread the whole record: the natural portals of the Bermuda Triangle’s ley-line convergences, the Skinwalker Ranch grid-point, the SSP’s jumpgates, and the portal-sealing defense shown live in the Unit X14 session. In the 2025 material the word even resurfaces in its governmental guise — “Project Stargate” — as an AI data-center program the sessions read darkly.

Doorway, temple, technique: the stargate is the archive’s connective tissue — the same geometry, it insists, whether carved in Khmer stone or opened behind closed eyes.
