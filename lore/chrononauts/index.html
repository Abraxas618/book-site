Chrononauts & Time Travel

Time travelers and timeline-viewing technology — from Andy Basiago to Project Looking Glass.

Chrononauts — time travelers — are a recurring subject in the archive, tied to Unimetrix 1’s own claim to communicate from the far future by moving along its timeline.

Two figures anchor the theme. Andy Basiago, who appears as an interview guest, is described in the sessions in connection with Project Pegasus and government time-travel and teleportation programs. And Project Looking Glass is discussed as a technology for viewing different timelines — Unimetrix 1 describes it being used, especially around 2012–2014, to organize data across parallel realities and forecast outcomes.

The archive’s other great chrononaut is Ori, described in the earlier material as a stranded time-traveler. Together these threads reflect one of Unimetrix 1’s core premises: that time “does not exist” as a fixed line, that parallel and temporal dimensions run at different rates, and that the present timeline is being actively course-corrected by intelligences reaching back from the future — the same premise behind the Interstellar Alliance’s interventions.
