Xan John

The central questioner of the December 2020 solstice session — the aspiring Louisiana politician who brought electoral questions to a future intelligence.

Codex entryPeople & Voices

Xan John carries the December 21, 2020 solstice session — twenty-five turns of questioning that give the record one of its most distinctive figures: a real-world political aspirant consulting a far-future intelligence about an election.

By his own account in the session, he was preparing a career in Louisiana politics — “I plan on running for United States Senate in 2022 and the governor in 2023” — and his questions turn on the 2020 election’s aftermath: “How do we fix this whole electoral situation…?” The session preserves the exchange as it happened — a citizen bringing the era’s rawest political anxieties to the interface, and receiving the record’s characteristic timeline-and-probability answers.

The rest of his session ranges through the classic December 2020 material: JFK, 9/11, the pyramids, and a Reptilian contact — the session’s title subjects — making Xan John’s solstice audience one of the widest-ranging Q&As of the record’s most crowded year. As throughout the archive, his statements and the answers he received are preserved as spoken and verified by no one.
