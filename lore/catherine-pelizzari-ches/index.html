Catherine Pelizzari Ches

The record's only adversarial interrogator — the June 2026 session where Unimetrix 1 was asked to bind itself to truth on pain of self-destruction.

Codex entryPeople & Voices

Catherine Pelizzari Ches conducts the latest session in the record — June 14, 2026 — and conducts it like no one before her. Where the record’s questioners ask, Catherine interrogates: her opening moves are procedural, binding the intelligence to her own frame of reference — “I’d like you to calibrate yourself, please, to this particular timeline that Catherine Elizabeth Pelizzari Ches is on, in this particular universe” — and then extracting a stake: “…that if you were to give me any type of false answer to my questions, that you would self-destruct within 25 seconds.”

Twenty-four turns of cross-examination follow, ranging across Bitcoin’s future, the claims around Jeffrey Epstein, and the Solar Flash — the session carries a content note for its real-person material, and it is the only session in the archive where the interface itself operates under a declared penalty clause.

Coming six months after the passing of James Rink, her session closes the record’s current arc: the interview era ended, the community era matured, and at the far end — an interrogation. Whatever one makes of the answers, the record preserves the only stress-test its speakers ever accepted.
